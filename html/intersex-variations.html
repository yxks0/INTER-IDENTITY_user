INTERSEX VARIATIONS

3 COMMON INTERSEX VARIATIONS

COMPLETE ANDROGEN INSENSITIVITY SYNDROME (CAIS)

People with complete AIS are born with female external genitalia and undescended testes. During puberty, they develop breasts due to the conversion of testosterone to estrogen but do not menstruate because they lack a uterus and ovaries.

Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (CAH)

CAH is a genetic condition that affects how adrenal glands produce hormones. It can lead to higher levels of androgens. In people with XX chromosomes, CAH can cause genital differences at birth and increased body hair and muscle during puberty.

Klinefelter Syndrome

Klinefelter syndrome is when someone has an extra X chromosome (47XXY). They may have smaller testes, low testosterone, delayed puberty, tall stature, breast tissue, and infertility. Many people with this condition may not know they have it.

Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome

People born with AIS have XY chromosomes and testes. Their bodies show varying degrees of insensitivity to androgens, including testosterone, meaning their cells don't respond normally to testosterone. AIS exists as a spectrum, with categories like complete (CAIS), partial (PAIS), and mild androgen insensitivity (MAIS).

Gonadal dysgenesis

Gonadal dysgenesis refers to conditions where the gonads (ovaries or testes) do not develop normally. This can lead to issues with hormone production and reproductive development.

5-ALPHA REDUCTASE DEFICIENCY

People with 5-alpha reductase deficiency (5-ARD) have XY chromosomes and testes but can't convert testosterone fully to DHT. This affects their genital development, causing differences at birth. During adolescence, they may develop some male puberty traits like increased muscle mass and a deeper voice, with possible changes in genital size.

MICROPENIS

A micropenis is smaller than typical at birth, often due to various intersex variations. However, its size typically does not affect its functionality.

TURNER SYNDROME

Turner Syndrome is a condition where individuals have a 45X chromosome pattern instead of the typical 46XX. It can vary (45X/46XX or 45X/46XY), affecting height, heart health, and sex characteristics like hormone production and puberty.

Kallmann Syndrome

Kallmann Syndrome is a type of hypogonadism affecting people with XX or XY chromosomes. It can cause genital differences at birth and delay puberty, requiring hormone therapy for secondary sexual characteristics. It also affects their sense of smell.
