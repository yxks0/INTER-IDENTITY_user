FAQs

What does intersex mean?

Intersex is a term used to describe a variety of conditions in which a person is born with reproductive or sexual anatomy that doesn't fit typical definitions of male or female.

How common is intersex?

Approximately 1.7% of the population is born with intersex traits, which is more than the population of Japan.

Is intersex the same as being transgender?

No, intersex refers to biological sex characteristics, while transgender relates to gender identity. An intersex person may identify as male, female, both, neither, or in another way.

How is intersex diagnosed?

Intersex can be diagnosed at birth, during puberty, or later in life through physical examinations, genetic testing, and hormone analyses.

What causes intersex traits?

Intersex traits can result from various genetic, hormonal, or developmental factors that affect sex differentiation.

Are there different types of intersex conditions?

Yes, there are many different intersex conditions, such as Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome (AIS), Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (CAH), and 5-Alpha-Reductase Deficiency.

Can intersex traits be treated or corrected?

Medical treatments are not always necessary. Decisions about medical intervention should be made on a case-by-case basis, prioritizing the individual's health, well-being, and informed consent.

Do intersex people have specific health concerns?

Intersex individuals may have unique health needs related to their specific condition, but they can lead healthy lives with appropriate medical care and support.

How should I refer to someone who is intersex?

Use the term "intersex" unless the person specifies a different preference. It's always respectful to ask someone how they identify and what terms they prefer.

Are intersex people infertile?

Fertility varies among intersex individuals. Some may be able to conceive children naturally, while others may face fertility challenges.

Can intersex people have normal sex lives?

Yes, intersex people can have fulfilling sex lives. Sexual function and experience vary among individuals, just like with anyone else.

What legal protections exist for intersex people?

Legal protections for intersex individuals vary by country and region. Advocacy continues to push for more inclusive laws and policies to protect intersex rights.

How can I support an intersex friend or family member?

Listen, educate yourself, and offer unconditional support. Respect their identity and choices, and advocate for their rights and well-being.

Are there intersex advocacy organizations?

Yes, there are many organizations worldwide that advocate for intersex rights, provide resources, and support intersex individuals and their families.

Where can I learn more about intersex?

Reliable information can be found through intersex advocacy groups, medical organizations, academic research, and educational resources dedicated to intersex issues.
